The Role of the Outside Hitter

The outside hitter is a key offensive player in volleyball, often referred to as the "primary attacker." They are expected to be versatile, playing both in the front and back rows, and are heavily involved in passing, hitting, and blocking.

Key Responsibilities

  • Primary attacker for the team, responsible for scoring points.
  • Plays both in the front and back row, requiring a mix of offensive and defensive skills.
  • Strong in passing and receiving serves.
  • Effective at blocking opponent's attacks.

Skills Needed

  • Strong hitting ability, especially from the outside position.
  • Excellent passing and serving skills.
  • Ability to read the game and anticipate plays from the opponent.

Tips for Aspiring Outside Hitters

  • Work on your timing and communication with your setter.
  • Enhance your ability to adjust to different sets and attacks.
  • Develop strong defensive skills, especially when receiving serves.
