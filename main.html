The Basics of Volleyball

Volleyball is a team sport in which two teams of six players each are separated by a net. The objective is to send the ball over the net into the opponent’s court to score points, while preventing the ball from going out of bounds or into your own court.

  • Each team has six players: three in the front row and three in the back row.
  • The game is typically played on a rectangular court that measures 18m x 9m.
  • Players use their hands or arms to hit the ball over the net, and the ball is allowed to touch the ground inside the boundaries of the court.

Volleyball Rules

Scoring System

Volleyball uses a rally point system, meaning points can be scored by either team, regardless of which team served.

Basic Rules

  • Each team has three hits to return the ball over the net.
  • Players cannot touch the net during play.
  • A player is not allowed to hit the ball twice consecutively.
  • The ball must be served from behind the back line.

Volleyball Tips

For Beginners

  • Practice your passing and serving techniques regularly.
  • Always keep your eyes on the ball and anticipate the opponent’s moves.
  • Work on your communication skills with teammates during the game.

For Advanced Players

  • Master the art of positioning and reading the game for effective defense.
  • Enhance your timing for jumping and blocking at the net.
  • Work on your ability to deliver powerful and accurate serves.

Volleyball Positions and Responsibilities

Opposite Hitter

Key offensive player who attacks from the right side of the court. Plays both offense and defense, and can also help in setting plays when needed.

Outside Hitter

Plays both in the front and back row. Primary attacker, responsible for passing, hitting, and blocking. Often the go-to hitter for the team.

Setter

Set up the ball for attacking players. The setter's job is to deliver accurate sets, often the leader of the offense.

Middle Blocker

Plays near the net and focuses on blocking the opponent’s attacks. Also a key player in quick offense plays like fast middle hits.

Libero

Specialized in passing, receiving serves, and playing defense. Libero cannot attack the ball above the net.

Defensive Specialist

Focuses on receiving serves and playing defense. Often comes in as a substitute for a front-row player to strengthen the defense.
